# OptivexIQ SaaS Profile

This document provides the recommended SaaS Profile entries for OptivexIQ based on the current product positioning, homepage narrative, and in-app profile structure.

## Recommended Profile

### ICP role

`Head of Product Marketing`

Why:

- Best fit for the current OptivexIQ positioning.
- Still supports Founder, VP Growth, and Revenue Leader as adjacent buyers.

Alternative acceptable values:

- `Founder`
- `VP Growth`
- `Revenue Leader`

### Primary pain

`Our team can see funnel outcomes, but we cannot clearly diagnose where messaging, pricing, and positioning create buyer friction. Homepage rewrites become opinion battles, differentiation weakens against competitors, and pricing pages fail to build enough confidence for serious evaluation.`

### Buying trigger

`We start evaluating a solution like OptivexIQ when traffic or pipeline intent exists, but conversion quality is weaker than expected, win confidence drops in competitive deals, or pricing and homepage messaging are no longer clearly supporting the sales motion.`

### Website URL

`https://YOUR_PRODUCTION_DOMAIN`

Use your real production domain here.

### ACV range

`10k_50k`

Why:

- Best default for a serious B2B SaaS workflow product.
- High enough to imply meaningful buyer scrutiny without overstating enterprise maturity.

### Revenue stage

`pre`

Why:

- Best default if OptivexIQ does not yet have active paying customers.

### Sales motion

`Founder-led sales with product-led entry and sales-assisted expansion`

Shorter acceptable version:

`Hybrid PLG and sales-assisted`

### Conversion goal

`demo`

Why:

- Best fit for the current product and buyer sophistication.
- Stronger than `trial` if the current motion depends on qualification and evaluation.

### Pricing model

`Tiered SaaS pricing with a free messaging snapshot, paid report and rewrite workflow plans, and sales-led team expansion`

## Key objections

Use these as the recommended entries:

- `Why not just use ChatGPT or Claude for messaging analysis and rewrites?`
- `How do we know this is a serious conversion intelligence platform and not an AI wrapper?`
- `Will this improve buyer confidence and conversion quality, or just generate nicer copy?`
- `Can this actually help us differentiate against competitors in active evaluations?`
- `Is the analysis credible enough for product marketing, growth, and revenue teams to use in decision-making?`
- `How does this fit our workflow without creating another disconnected tool?`

## Proof points

Use these as the recommended entries:

- `Structured conversion intelligence workflow that starts with messaging diagnostics, competitive gap analysis, and rewrite review instead of one-off prompt outputs.`
- `Report-first system that surfaces overlap, missing proof, pricing friction, and objection coverage before suggesting rewrites.`
- `Built specifically for B2B SaaS teams making positioning, pricing, and conversion decisions in competitive markets.`
- `Rewrite Studio supports comparison and review workflows rather than auto-publishing unvetted AI copy.`
- `The platform is explicit that outputs are directional guidance for prioritization and testing, not guaranteed results.`
- `The Confidence Gap Framework gives OptivexIQ a named methodology for translating messaging and pricing signals into decision-ready priorities.`

## Differentiation matrix

### Competitor: ChatGPT / Claude

Our advantage:

`OptivexIQ diagnoses messaging, pricing, proof, and objection gaps inside a structured conversion intelligence workflow instead of starting from a blank prompt.`

Their advantage:

`Broader general-purpose generation, more flexible open-ended prompting, and lower-friction ad hoc use.`

### Competitor: Traditional CRO analytics tools

Our advantage:

`OptivexIQ explains buyer-facing narrative friction and competitive overlap, not just behavioral outcomes or funnel metrics.`

Their advantage:

`Stronger quantitative funnel tracking, experimentation infrastructure, and behavioral analytics depth.`

### Competitor: Messaging consultants / agencies

Our advantage:

`OptivexIQ creates a repeatable internal workflow for diagnostics, prioritization, and rewrite evaluation instead of relying only on one-off external strategic engagements.`

Their advantage:

`Human judgment, custom strategic facilitation, and richer cross-functional advisory support.`

### Competitor: AI copywriting tools

Our advantage:

`OptivexIQ is positioned around conversion intelligence and reviewable decision artifacts rather than fast headline generation or generic landing-page copy.`

Their advantage:

`Faster lightweight drafting for teams that only want copy generation and not deeper strategic analysis.`
